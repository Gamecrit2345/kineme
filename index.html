Volunteer Lawyers Backing Up Impeach Team

By: Gabriel Pabico Lalu, Kenneth Christiane Basilio — Philippine Daily Inquirer
June 12, 2026
MANY ARE WILLING: Batangas Rep. Gerville Luistro (left) introduces veteran litigator Lorna Kapunan (right) as one of the volunteer private prosecutors. — Screengrab from ABS-CBN NEWS
MANILA, Philippines — The House prosecution panel has enlisted top volunteer lawyers for the upcoming impeachment trial of Vice President Sara Duterte.

Instead of the “bloodbath” she claimed she was eager for when she was first impeached last year, Vice President Sara Duterte now seems to prefer a “bubble bath,” based on her response to the charges, a private prosecutor in her imminent trial said on Thursday.

Veteran litigator Lorna Kapunan, one of the 10 volunteer lawyers assisting the 11-member House prosecution panel, supported the prosecutors’ view that the defense is trying to evade a full hearing on the merits.

“If we remember, she said before… that there would be a bloodbath that would occur. But where is that bloodbath? What I can see, personally, is that it is a bubble bath, not a bloodbath.” — Atty. Lorna Kapunan

Key Points

  • Defense answer allegedly contains only rehashed technical arguments and old annexes.
  • Core charges remain the same: culpable violation of the Constitution, betrayal of public trust, graft and corruption, bribery, and high crimes.
  • Second impeachment supported by 257 House members (vs 215 in the first).
  • Trial is set to begin on July 6, 2026.

Volunteer Private Prosecutors

The volunteer lawyers come from top law firms and are serving pro bono:

  • Kapunan and Castillo Law: Atty. Lorna Kapunan, Sonya Margarita Benemerito Castillo, Deanna Melissa Lorenzo Singian, Lino Chris Kapunan, Bettina Zamora
  • Pecabar Law: F Marforth Fua, Erwin Matib, Justine Anne Lamarca, James Bryan Ibrahim Alih
  • SaLiGal Law: Atty. Amando Ligutan

Batangas Rep. Gerville Luistro, head of the House prosecution panel, expressed confidence in the “abogado de campanilla” joining the team.

Prosecution Position

The House has filed a manifestation stating there is no need to reply to Duterte’s answer because it did not address the material allegations. They submitted their pre-trial brief on June 15.
